Some notes on cell experiments

Passaging cells

  1. Observe the cell morphology under a microscope and enter the experimental operation table after disinfection

  2. ‚ÄúUndressing‚Äù: Pour out the original culture solution first

  3. ‚ÄúBath‚Äù: 2ml PBS to wash cell metabolites, discard after shaking, and repeat again

  4. Add 1ml of trypsin to digest the cells, then put them in the incubator for 1-2 minutes. Observe the cell morphology under the microscope, until the cells appear in suspension, then quickly take back to the operating table, add 1~2ml DMEM, and rinse repeatedly. Wash all the cells attached to the culture flask into the solution

  5. Centrifuge for 5 minutes (take a test tube with a similar amount of solution to balance)

  6. At the end of centrifugation, use a pipette to suck up the upper layer of solution, add 4ml of DMEM to rinse repeatedly, then pour it into the culture flask, shake back and forth + left and right to make the cells evenly distributed

  7. Label, put the culture flask back into the incubator
